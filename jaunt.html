Jaunt

For those eager to enjoy an evening out or discover a new city without the hassle of planning, this app will be perfect solution. It will simplify the planning process, offering users a tailored itinerary with just a few user perferences or a comprehensive plan based on their current location. Whether it's due to lack of time, a dislike for planning, or a desire for new experiences, the Jaunt app will ensure a seamless and enjoyable exploration.

Try the Prototype

Hypothesis

For many, the thought of meticulously planning every detail of a day or evening out is overwhelming, leading them to revert to familiar plans or, in some cases, to forgo outings entirely. An application that simplifies this process by swiftly organizing all elements of an outing, while also offering the flexibility of easy modifications, would be greatly welcomed and in demand.

User Research

I interviewed users to learn about their trip planning experiences and challenges. Their responses, gathered through open-ended questions, provided critical insights for the user-centered design of this location-based recommendation app.

User questions

  • Are you someone who enjoys qoing out on day trips or out in the evenings?
  • How do you use websites or apps to help you plan a trip out and If you don’t what are the main reasons why?
  • What features of any existing app did you find the most useful and why?
  • Regarding the websites and applications you utilize, which functionalities do you consider to be superfluous, and can you share your reasons for this perspective?
  • If an app/website made a suggestion on where to go what would be the biggest factor on making you agree with it and why?
  • How often during the day out would you have to go back into the app/website to re-plan and what are the main reasons why?
  • If there is an app designed for helping people plan a day out what features do you think would be essential and why?

Patterns

  • Everyone liked to be able to navigate the map with ease
  • People like to be able to check the places before hand either via reviews or the photos

Frustrations

  • Lack of accuracy seemed to be the most frustrating issue for people.

Surprises

  • I guess the most surprising thing was the fact that Google Maps isn’t the best at getting you to the right level, it just gets you close enough which works for most places but not something in say a basement or rooftop.

User Personas

Based on user testing results, I developed user personas that provided a comprehensive understanding of my target audience. These personas, detailing user frustrations, goals, and motivations, served as a constant reference throughout the app's development, ensuring the final product aligned with user needs.

User-Flow

Following the user testing and persona development, I proceeded to create the user flow. This process provided a deeper understanding of how to optimize the web-based app's structure to better align with user needs and wants, while also maintaining a familiar and intuitive design.

Wireframes

When designing my wireframes, I began with the 'Crazy Eights' technique, which helped me rapidly generate initial ideas. From there, I refined my preferred design into these low-fidelity paper wireframes.

Once I was fully satisfied with my low-fidelity wireframes, I transferred them into Figma and developed them into the mid-fidelity wireframes seen below. From there, I was able to focus more easily on how all the key elements interacted on the mobile screen, while also ensuring a strong informational hierarchy.

Style guide

With my wireframes finalized, I began creating a style guide for the app. Much like the user personas helped me easily refer back to the users' wants and needs, the style guide helped me develop a unified style that I could readily refer to during the app's development.

As this was a location-based recommendation app, I wanted it to evoke a light, outdoorsy feel. Therefore, I chose yellows and greens for the primary colors, complemented by a dark blue for the text and borders, ensuring strong readability overall.

Conclusion

The UX/UI case study for our location-based app, JAUNT, has been insightful and rewarding. By focusing on user-centered design, I've created a platform that simplifies day trip planning, making it more enjoyable and efficient.

Challenges I faced included selecting the appropriate color palette for this type of app and designing a simple, intuitive layout that worked seamlessly across multiple screens.

Next Iteration

In the next iteration, I would add the following features:

  • Fully implemented search filters
  • An E-ticket and booking menu to steamline users' journeys.
  • A weather feature that notifies users of changing conditions and provides alternative plans.
